How the Internet Works

This site will give you a brief background of how the internet works.

The Internet is, formally, "... a loosely-organized international collaboration of autonomous, interconnected networks, supports host-to-host communication through voluntary adherence to open protocols and procedures defined by Internet Standards." A more casual definition is, "A collection of multiple different pieces of computer and non-computer equipment talking to one another by means of a communications network either wired or wireless."

Most people that use the internet every single day have no idea where it came from or how it works! In the 1970's, Bob Kahn and Vint Cerf began working on the design of what we now call the internet. It actually came out of another project called ARPANET, or "Advanced Research Projects Agency Network". This was a project for the government in which Paul Baran was trying to develop a communications network that would actually survive a nuclear attack. They had the idea of breaking messages into blocks and sending them as fast as possible in every direction through the mesh network. They built a nationwide network, and it worked!

There is no one person or entity that is in control of the internet. The internet is made up of a large number of independently owned networks and there is no central control. All operators are motivated to assure that there is connectivity to all ends of the network which ensures for great utility.

This website will teach you about the tangible components that make up the internet and will describe how they work. Please read and enjoy!
